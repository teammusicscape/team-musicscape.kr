About the Work

By the Sea of Gampo was composed after visiting sites connected to King Munmu of Silla, whose legacy remains deeply embedded in the coastal landscape of Gampo. After unifying the Three Kingdoms, the king is said to have wished to rest in the sea, vowing to protect the nation even after death. This spirit—anchored in the East Sea—forms the historical and emotional ground of the piece. The work also resonates with the legend of Manpasikjeok, a mythical flute believed to calm all turmoil and bring peace.

The composer originally imagined the piece for daegeum, but later reworked it as a duo for guitar and piri, discovering a new resonance in the process. The clear tone of the piri recalls the breath of wind and waves, while the deep resonance of the guitar carries echoes of land and sea. Through their dialogue, past and present, tradition and contemporary sound are woven together, calling forth a wish for calm and balance.

When the piece was written in 2017, society was marked by tension and division. Standing before the sea at Gampo, the composer held a quiet hope that noise and unrest might subside, and that peace and collective well-being could return. That hope remains embedded in the music, carried by the steady movement of breath and sound.
