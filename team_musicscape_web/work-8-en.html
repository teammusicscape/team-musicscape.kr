About the Work

Island began from impressions gathered while traveling among islands along Korea’s west coast. From a distance, these isolated islands appeared solitary and remote. Approaching them, however, revealed a different reality: though the paths were rough, the landscapes were rich with unexpected beauty and vibrant life.

Within this contrast, the composer began to ask what other distant places might still remain within our own hearts—places not yet reached, not yet understood. Island traces a journey from loneliness, unfamiliarity, and preconceived distance toward new understanding and quiet beauty. The piece was composed during the period of the COVID-19 pandemic, when many experienced a sense of isolation similar to that of an island. In a time when approaching one another felt increasingly difficult, the music reflects both solitude and resilience. Through the imagery of island landscapes, the work sings of living vitality within isolation, and of a world of possibility that still waits beyond what has been experienced.
